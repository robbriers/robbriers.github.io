Rob Briers

Ecologist and Lecturer

Edinburgh Napier University

I am an ecologist and lecturer in the School of Applied Sciences, Edinburgh Napier University.

I have a particular interest in aquatic ecosystems, quantitative methods (I’m a bit of a R nerd) and all things spatial. I investigate a wide range of pure and applied ecological questions and employ a combination of laboratory, field and modelling approaches to my research.

Details of some of my current research interests can be found in the Projects section. I am always interested in hearing from potential research students, so if you want to get in touch, see the Contact information.

Teaching

I teach across a range of subjects, at both undergraduate and postgraduate levels. I have a particular interest in Technology Enhanced Learning. Since we adopted Moodle as our VLE, I have also been spending some time hacking together HTML and scripts to develop resources for both students and academic staff. If you are interested in Stats teaching, check out my popular StatsTV series of videos for Minitab. These will be superceded by R versions soon as we revise our undergraduate stats teaching.
