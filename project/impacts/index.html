Anthropogenic impacts

 

Underwater noise effects

Increasing levels of underwater noise resulting from anthropogenic sources are an emerging source of pollution. I have recently become interested in the effects of this on invertebrates in marine ecosystems. This work is being undertaken in conjunction with Dr Karen Diele and other collaborators, with some of the practical work being undertaken at the St Abbs Marine Station.

Diffuse pollution and urban drainage

Diffuse pollution is a widespread problem in freshwater ecosysterms and my work has involved examining and predicting the impacts of diffuse pollution in freshwaters and evaluating the role of constructed wetlands (SUDS and similar approaches in rural areas) in alleviating these issues and supporting biodiversity goals. See Briers 2014.
